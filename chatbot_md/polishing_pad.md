**What is a Medium Cut Pad?**

In the world of paint correction, pads hierarchy generally goes from heavy cut, to medium cut, and finally to finishing.

- Heavy Cut Pads: Designed for aggressive defect removal (deep scratches, heavy oxidation), often paired with coarse compounds.
- Medium Cut Pads: Positioned in the middle, they are designed to remove moderate defects using less aggressive polishes, leaving a much better finish than heavy cut pads.
- Finishing Pads: Used with fine polishes or glazes to refine the finish, remove holograms or marring left by previous steps, and maximise gloss.

The Menzerna Medium Cut Foam Pad embodies this middle ground perfectly. It possesses enough cutting power to eliminate annoying swirls and light imperfections but is gentle enough to minimize marring and finish down well, often reducing the subsequent steps required.

- Durable Construction: Built to withstand the demands of machine polishing, the pad resists tearing and breakdown, offering reliable performance over multiple uses and cleaning cycles.

## How To Use

**General usage notes:**

(i) Always select the least aggressive pad you think you will need to achieve the desired result, and step up one grade at a time if more cut is required.
(ii) Always ensure that you centre each pad properly on the backing plate (this can take several attempts to get right, but is vital if the machine is to remain perfectly balanced during use).
(iii) Always use an appropriate sized pad for the backing plate you are using (the pad should always protrude past the edge of the backing plate, never the other way around).
(iv) Always prime a new pad by loading it with 2-6 pea-sized drops of product (depending on pad size) and running it stationary on the paint for 30 seconds at speed 1.
(v) Always ensure that spent polish residue on the face of the pad is brushed off regularly during use using a pad brush, such as RUPES BigFoot Claw Pad Tool (failure to follow this advice can lead to micromarring of the finish).
(vi) After use, clean the pads with warm water and mild soap (using your fingertips to massage out any remaining polish residue), rinse with running water and allow to dry naturally.
(vii) While not in use, store in a dry, dust-free environment (lidded plastic storage boxes are ideal in this respect).

**Tips for success:**

- Do not over-saturate the pad with compound. Too much compound can lead to diminished performance.
- Avoid the use of solvent based compounds and cleaners to prevent damage to the foam cell structure.

[What kind of polishing pads are available?](javascript:;)

- Pad with a diameter of 150 mm: 5 pea-sized drops

Approximately 20 cars can be polished with Menzerna’s premium pads. However, the polishing pads need to be thoroughly cleaned. The Menzerna Premium Pads are available in different sizes:

## FAQ - Questions & answers

**What kind of polishing pads are available?**
Polishing pads are generally divided into foam pads, microfiber pads, and lambswool polishing pads. Menzerna offers foam polishing pads in various degrees of hardness as well as a variety of lambswool pads for orbital and rotary polishing machines. Polishing pads come in different sizes.

**How much polish should be applied to the polishing pad?**
Recommendation for Menzerna polishes and a surface measuring 40 cm x 40 cm:
- Pad with a diameter of 95 mm: 3 to 4 pea-sized drops
- Pad with a diameter of 150 mm: 5 pea-sized drops
- Pad with a diameter of 180 mm: 6 to 7 pea-sized drops

**How many cars can I polish with the Menzerna pads?**
Approximately 20 cars can be polished with Menzerna's premium pads. However, the polishing pads need to be thoroughly cleaned.

**Which pads should I use for Menzerna polishes?**
Menzerna's premium pads are color-coordinated with the polish labels. The recommended polishing accessories can be found on each product's detail page on our website.

**What are the options for cleaning a polishing pad?**
Foam polishing pads can be washed by hand with clear water. Alternatively, a bucket may also be used. Please note: Lambswool polishing pads should never be rinsed with water. Instead, they should be cleaned with a lambswool brush or an air blow gun.

### Sıkça Sorulan Sorular
**S: KP160-22L (Agresif) ile farkı nedir?**
C: KP160-22KL standart kuzu postu keçesidir, KP160-22L ise daha agresif yapıya sahiptir ve daha sert verniklerde kullanılır.

**S: Neden Merinos kuzu postu?**
C: 1. Sınıf Merinos kuzu postu en yüksek kesim gücünü ve dayanıklılığı sağlar.

**S: Yüzeyi aşırı ısıtır mı?**
C: Hayır, rotary hareket ile hava tüyler arasında sirküle olur ve yüzeyin aşırı ısınmasını engeller.

**S: Far plastiklerinde kullanılır mı?**
C: Evet, tüm boyalı yüzeyler ve far bölgelerinde kullanılabilir.
